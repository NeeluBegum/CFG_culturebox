France

Famous Authors

Guy de Maupassant

This 19th century French writer is best-known for being the master of short stories. One of his most famous and internationally-acclaimed books is "Boule de Suif", although we would also highly recommend "Bel Ami".

Colette

Nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1948, Colette was an actress, journalist, novelist, and (interestingly) a French mime. Made a Grand Officier de la Légion d'Honneur (one of the highest honours in France) in 1953, she is best known for her novel "Gigi", but also for "Claudine à l'école" (Claudine at School), which was one of her first novels (published in 1900). One of her greatest quotations was : "You will do foolish things, but do them with enthusiasm". If you're interested, a live-action film about her life (where she is protrayed by Keira Knigthley) recently came out.

Good food

Boeuf Bourguignon

This stew is perfect for winter. Usually made with beef, red wine, onions, and carrots, it was first documented in the 19th century and has become a staple of Parisian bistrots.

Singers

Matthieu Chédid

Better known as "-M-" (his stage name), Matthieu Chédid comes from a very artistic family and has been performing hits since the late 1990s. His more well-known songs include "Je dis aime", "Onde Sensuelle", and "Mama Sam". He recently announced the release of his newest album ("Lettre infinie"), which will be released in 2019.
